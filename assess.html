    • Assess: Program Audit

    I assess current programs based on how likely they are to impact change. I identify strengths and weaknesses of each program being considered through observing the actual program in practice and interviews/surveys with staff. Understanding both how the program was designed and how it really looks in practice is key to addressing design-practice gaps.
    When interviewing or surveying staff, I am looking for:
    Who designs the programs?
    Who do they collaborate with?
    What is the goal of the program?
    How is the goal intended to be achieved?
    How is it being approached (strategy)?
    What theory(ies) supports the strategy?
    What applied tools are being used to achieve the goal?
    How are staff being trained to deliver the program?
    When observing programs, I consider:
    Does the design translate to practice?
    What theory/applied tools are actually being used?
    Are they used appropriately?
    Are the current strategies effective?
    What are the strengths of the program? Weaknesses?
    What theoretical models can I identify?
    Once sufficient information is gathered, I will analyze the findings and assimilate them into a report. The report will include recommendations for increasing the effectiveness of your program(s).
